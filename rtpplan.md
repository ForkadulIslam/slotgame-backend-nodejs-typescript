Bankroll Management: Practical Guidelines
Mathematics doesn’t care about your feelings, and slots are negative expectation games. You will lose money over time if you play enough. The question is whether you extract entertainment value commensurate with that cost.

Session budgeting: For medium volatility JILI games, start with 100-150x your intended bet size as a session bankroll. If you’re betting RM1 per spin, bring RM100-150. This gives you runway to hit a feature or two before depleting. For high volatility games, increase to 200-300x. Charge Buffalo at RM1 per spin needs RM200-300 to have realistic feature hit probability.

Bet sizing: Never exceed 1-2% of your total gambling bankroll on a single spin. If you have RM1,000 allocated to slots this month, individual spins should stay at RM10-20 maximum. This isn’t fun-killing caution, it’s survival mathematics. Variance will destroy improperly sized bets.

Win goals and loss limits: Set both before you start. Common approach: walk away at 50% loss of session bankroll (you brought RM150, you leave at RM75) or 100% profit (RM150 becomes RM300, you withdraw and walk). These are arbitrary but create circuit breakers against tilt.

Feature chasing is a trap: The human brain convinces you that a bonus is “due” after 250 dead spins on a game with 165-spin average trigger frequency. Probability doesn’t work that way. Each spin is an independent event. The game doesn’t owe you anything, and “one more spin” compounds losses faster than any other behavior pattern.
